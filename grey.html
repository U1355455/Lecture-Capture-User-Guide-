Lecture Capture - User Guide

Learn how to use tools & features within Lecture Capture!

Lecture Capture is an upcoming software that is being introduced within Higher Education. The University of Huddersfield has recently introduced the software campus wide and this user guide explains everything you need to know to use the software effectively to get the best from it.

What is Lecture Capture?

The software uses audio-visual equipment to capture the contents of the lecture as it is taking place. The AV equipment makes it possible to record both sound and image within a lecture, it may be that the whole lecture is recorded or just the screen, depending on the preference of the lecturer.

The service provider for the University of Huddersfield is Panopto so please don’t be confused if you see this mentioned around campus, although the software should always be branded as HudStream.

What can Lecture Capture be used for?

Lecture Capture recordings are useful to go over the content that has been mentioned in a lecture again. It may be that the academic has gone over the information quickly and the student missed an important aspect within the lecture. It could be that a student has a valid reason to not attend a lecture such as ill health, Lecture Capture can insure that the student won’t miss the content of a lecture if they were unable to attend. It is possible that a student struggles to engage in a lecture and take notes at the same time they may find it useful to watch the recording later and take notes from the recording so that they are able to fully engage in the session.

When is the Lecture Capture recording available?

The Lecture Capture recording will be made available to students 48 hours after the lecture has taken place, this allows the lecturer to edit the recording or make any changes, include notes etc. If there are no changes to be made to the recording then it may be that it is made available immediately. Students can then visit the capture as many times as they wish.

What devices can the videos be accessed on?

The Lecture Capture videos are compatible with all devices, which means that students can use their PC, Laptop, Smartphone or Tablet to watch the videos. The videos are streamed rather than downloaded, which means the device will either use Wi-Fi or a data plan.

How can Lecture Capture be accessed?

Once the capture is uploaded there are two ways in which a student can access the Lecture Capture recordings. Either through UniLearn or by directly logging into HudStream.

UniLearn

Lecture Capture can be accessed through UniLearn, once logged into Lecture Capture recording for each module can be found under each module tab. The videos will be stored within the folder in each module in date order.

HudStream

Another way of accessing Lecture Capture recordings is logging in directly through HudStream.

Contents:

The contents tab within Lecture Capture can be used by the Lecturer to give a brief overview or a detailed summary of the contents of a lecture captured.

Notes

Notes can be added to the Lecture Capture video whilst you are watching, this feature can be used generally for taking notes on the contents of the lecture.

Bookmarks

Bookmarks can be added to specific points of a lecture, maybe at a point you know you will need to return to in the future for revision.

Discussion

The discussion tool can be used by all joined to the module, this can be useful if you need to ask your lecturer something you know will be useful to fellow students or you would like the opinion of your fellow students. Please note if you have an important question it may be better to contact the lecturer directly as this tool may not be something they check regularly.

Buttons

There are several buttons along the bottom off the player within HudStream, these can be used for different actions.

The first button allows you to rewind by ten seconds if something that has been mentioned is important and needs replaying.

The next is the volume button which allows you to adjust the volume of the video.

The next button is the speed button, this allows you to adjust the speed of the video, you can either speed it up or slow it down.

Next to this is the quality button, this allows you to adjust the quality of the video to your preferred quality.

Then there is the camera button, this allows you to change the view of the video by switching between cameras.

The last button on the player, which is the hide button allows you to show or hide the frames below, depending on your preferred view.
